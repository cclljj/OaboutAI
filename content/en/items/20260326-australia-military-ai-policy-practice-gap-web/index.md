## Detailed Notes

- The policy arrives during a period of intensified military AI use in active conflict zones.
- Australia sets three core requirements: lawful use, accountable use, and proportionate risk controls.
- Human accountability and harm-aware design are emphasized as foundational governance principles.
- Testing/training/evaluation are identified as controls, but concrete methods are under-specified.
- Article 36 legal review commitment is a notable differentiator and potential governance strength.
- Coverage is broad, spanning from narrow tools to frontier general-purpose AI models.
- Implementation details across Army, Navy, Air Force, and related entities remain unclear.
- The Defence AI Centre is identified as a governance hub, but monitoring/reporting pathways are not detailed.
- Compared with UK/US frameworks, Australia provides fewer operational roadmaps and maturity mechanisms.
- The policy’s real impact will depend on follow-on guidance, resourcing, and verifiable compliance practice.
