## Detailed Notes

- **Dataset window and context:** The report analyzes Claude usage from Feb 5–12, 2026, following Opus 4.5 and around the release of Opus 4.6.
- **Diversification signal:** Claude.ai traffic became less concentrated, with the top 10 tasks accounting for a smaller share than in November 2025.
- **Shift in task mix:** Growth in everyday personal-use queries lowered average wage-proxy value of tasks performed on Claude.ai.
- **Adoption-curve interpretation:** The pattern aligns with diffusion dynamics where early adopters focus on high-value specialist tasks and later users broaden usage categories.
- **Job-task penetration:** The report notes substantial occupational exposure, with many jobs having meaningful portions of tasks performed using Claude.
- **Global inequality persistence:** Per-capita usage remains skewed toward top countries, indicating uneven international diffusion.
- **Within-US convergence nuance:** State-level concentration eased slightly, suggesting diffusion is broadening domestically even as global gaps persist.
- **Learning curves as core mechanism:** More experienced users appear to realize higher value from the same underlying model ecosystem.
- **Model-task matching behavior:** Users choose higher-intelligence models more for higher-wage/complex tasks (e.g., coding), especially in API contexts.
- **Tenure-linked behavioral shift:** High-tenure users have fewer personal conversations and relatively higher-education task profiles.
- **Performance difference:** Higher-tenure cohorts show a ~10% conversation success advantage beyond task-selection effects.
- **Possible causal interpretation:** Findings are consistent with learning-by-doing, though selective early-adopter sophistication may also contribute.
- **Policy relevance:** Benefit capture may be reinforced by usage skill accumulation, not only by model access.
- **Labor-market implication:** AI literacy may evolve from binary access issue to gradient of practical competency and workflow fluency.
- **Organizational implication:** Firms should treat AI adoption as capability-building programs with coaching, not mere license provisioning.
- **Measurement implication:** Productivity analysis should track user tenure, task complexity, and success outcomes longitudinally.
- **Strategic takeaway:** The distribution of AI gains will likely depend on who learns fastest to operationalize model capability in real tasks.
