## Detailed Notes

- Establishes a policy vocabulary for “AI openness” and distinguishes levels/forms of openness across data, model artifacts, documentation, and access pathways.
- Frames openness decisions as policy trade-offs: innovation diffusion and accountability benefits versus misuse, security, and systemic-risk exposure.
- Emphasizes governance mechanisms for staged or conditional openness, including safeguards, documentation, and institutional oversight.
- Useful for regulators and ministries building proportionate AI policy that avoids both blanket openness and blanket restriction.
