## Detailed Notes

- Defines AI assurance as an ecosystem function across the value chain, including evaluators, standards bodies, deployers, and oversight actors.
- Warns that assurance concentrated only before launch is insufficient for high-impact systems that continue learning or acting in dynamic settings.
- Identifies low deployer demand for independent assurers as a core bottleneck, despite growing model risk exposure.
- Attributes weak demand to unclear regulatory expectations, IP confidentiality concerns, and limited understanding of external assurance value.
- Reports workshop sentiment favoring policy levers such as legislation, incident reporting, and transparency registers to stimulate assurance demand.
- Argues that frontier model risks (including high-consequence misuse domains) require trusted independent pre-deployment testing.
- Recommends adaptive process standards over rigid technical checklists so assurance can keep pace with capability shifts.
- Highlights agentic AI as a standards lag zone where voluntary multistakeholder frameworks must temporarily fill governance gaps.
- Emphasizes real-time failure detection and continuous post-deployment monitoring as critical for systems performing autonomous actions.
- Positions “justified trust” as evidence-backed confidence grounded in verifiable assurance practice rather than branding or self-attestation.
