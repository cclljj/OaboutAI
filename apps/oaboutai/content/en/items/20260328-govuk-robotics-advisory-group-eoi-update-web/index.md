## Detailed Notes

- DSIT marked the application window as closed and shifted focus to group operation.
- The RAG is positioned as an independent advisory mechanism for robotics policy.
- It replaces the earlier Robotics Growth Partnership, signaling institutional transition.
- Membership design emphasizes cross-ecosystem expertise (industry, academia, end users).
- Quarterly cadence plus ad hoc work suggests ongoing policy-support capacity.
- Governance expectations include due diligence and Nolan Principles alignment.
- Service limits and non-remuneration model indicate public-interest framing.
- The body can improve policy continuity through structured evidence inputs.
- Practical impact will depend on agenda quality and uptake by decision-makers.
- This is a relevant AI-policy signal because robotics governance increasingly overlaps with AI deployment risk.
