## Detailed Notes

- JingChuang-26 is scheduled for official launch in Q3 2026 and is treated as a national compute milestone.
- National supercomputing capacity is expected to rise significantly, enabling larger AI and HPC workloads.
- Taiwan’s strategy is evolving toward continuous annual upgrades instead of infrequent large refreshes.
- The platform combines H200 and GB200 architectures for differentiated workload optimization.
- Compute governance is explicit: capacity is planned across academia, government, and industry tracks.
- Public-sector compute gaps can create dependency on external foundation models with governance risk.
- Vendor lock-in is identified as a strategic concern for national compute resilience.
- Opening resources to SMEs and startups lowers barriers to applied AI innovation.
- Domain-specific LLM development (e.g., finance, legal, defense) is highlighted as a core near-term direction.
- NCHC’s role is expanding from research support to dual-purpose public governance and industrial enablement.
- JingChuang-27 planning indicates policy continuity and infrastructure lifecycle management.
- The case links AI governance directly to compute sovereignty, allocation policy, and security-aware deployment capacity.
