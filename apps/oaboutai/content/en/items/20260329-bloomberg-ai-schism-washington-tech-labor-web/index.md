## Detailed Notes

- Washington is in a policy-construction phase, not a stable AI regulatory end-state.
- Tech and labor coalitions are contesting regulatory sequencing and enforcement strength.
- Priority conflicts center on innovation, labor impact, and accountability allocation.
- Short-term legal uncertainty raises compliance planning costs for AI adopters.
- Sectoral rules may emerge before comprehensive federal harmonization.
- Organizations need adaptive governance and scenario-based compliance planning.
- Public-consultation channels can materially influence final rule design.
- Governance fragmentation risk should be treated as a strategic policy variable.
