## Detailed Notes

- Sacks stated his SGE time cap was exhausted, ending the prior White House role.
- He moved to PCAST co-chair, with a remit centered on advice rather than federal coordination.
- This implies a governance shift from directive execution to advisory influence.
- Policy throughput could slow, but portfolio scope across technology domains may expand.
- Institutional design (role type, authority, term limits) is shown to shape AI policy outcomes.
- Cross-agency alignment may now depend more on OSTP and executive-office mediation.
- Stakeholders should track whether recommendations translate into implementable directives.
- The transition may alter near-term federal prioritization across AI and crypto issues.
- Political accountability pathways can change when authority moves from operator to advisor.
- The case is a reminder that governance capacity is partly organizational architecture.
